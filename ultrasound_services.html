Ultrasound Services

Providing clear, compassionate, and high-quality diagnostic imaging to support your health and well-being.

Ultrasound, also known as sonography, is a safe and painless diagnostic procedure that uses sound waves to create images of the inside of the body. At CBH Clinic, we use state-of-the-art technology to provide crucial insights for obstetrics, gynecology, and general health monitoring.

Our Ultrasound Services

Comprehensive care for every stage of life.

Whole Abdomen

A comprehensive scan of all major abdominal organs, including the liver, gallbladder, spleen, pancreas, and kidneys.

view_cozy

Upper/Lower Abdomen

Focused examination of either the upper or lower regions of your abdomen for specific concerns.

water_drop

KUB (Kidneys, Ureters & Bladder)

A detailed look at the urinary system to detect issues like kidney stones or bladder abnormalities.

bubble_chart

HBT (Hepatobiliary Tree)

Specialized imaging of the liver, bile ducts, and gallbladder to assess function and detect blockages.

male

Scrotal Ultrasound

A diagnostic tool used to examine the testicles and surrounding tissues for lumps, swelling, or pain.

pulmonology

Chest Ultrasound

An imaging test to check for fluid around the lungs or other abnormalities within the chest cavity.

female

Pelvic Ultrasound

An examination of the female reproductive organs, including the uterus, ovaries, and bladder.

search

Transvaginal/Transrectal

An internal ultrasound providing clearer, more detailed images of the pelvic organs.

child_care

Congenital Anomaly Scan

A detailed mid-pregnancy scan to check for the healthy development of your baby's organs and structure.

cardiology

OB & Gyne Doppler

An advanced ultrasound that assesses blood flow in the umbilical cord, fetus, and placenta.

view_in_ar

3D/4D/5D Ultrasound

Advanced imaging that provides lifelike, three-dimensional, and real-time moving images of your baby.

science

SISH / HSSG

Specialized procedures to evaluate the uterine cavity and fallopian tubes for infertility investigations.

Preparing for Your Appointment

To ensure the best possible results from your ultrasound, preparation may be required. This can include fasting or having a full bladder. Please follow the specific instructions provided to you by our staff when you book your appointment. If you have any questions, don't hesitate to call us.
